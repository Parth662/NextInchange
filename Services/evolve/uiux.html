INSIDE OUR UX AUDIT PROCESS
Finding what's broken, understanding why, and figuring out what to fix first

Stakeholder interview

01

Understanding your goals and what’s not working

We start by talking to your team about what prompted the audit. What are users complaining about? Where are people dropping off? What business goals isn’t the product meeting? This gives us context for everything we find later.

Key steps:

  • Business objectives and pain points
  • Current metrics and problem areas
  • Technical constraints and limitations
  • Success criteria for improvements

Deliverables

Stakeholder summary audit scope document

Analytics review

02

Seeing where users struggle through the data

We dig into your analytics to find patterns. High bounce rates on specific pages, abandoned flows, and features nobody uses. The numbers tell us where to look deeper during the usability review.

Key steps:

  • Traffic and conversion analysis
  • User flow and drop-off tracking
  • Heatmaps and session recordings
  • Performance metrics review

Deliverables

Metrics report with flagged issues

User & competitor research

03

Understanding who you’re designing for and how you compare

We analyze your target audience and how they behave, then look at how competitors solve similar problems. This shows gaps in your experience and opportunities you’re missing.

Key steps:

  • User demographic and behaviour analysis
  • Competitor UX review
  • Industry pattern research
  • Best practice benchmarking

Deliverables

Audience report competitive analysis

Heuristic evaluation

04

Reviewing your product against usability principles

We go through your product page by page, evaluating it against established usability heuristics. Navigation clarity, visual hierarchy, error handling, accessibility. We document everything that violates best practices or creates friction.

Key steps:

  • Navigation and information architecture review
  • Visual design and hierarchy assessment
  • Interaction patterns evaluation
  • Accessibility compliance check

Deliverables

Heuristic evaluation report with screenshots

User flow analysis

05

Identifying where key journeys break down

We map critical user flows (signup, checkout, core features) and pinpoint exactly where things go wrong. Confusing labels, missing feedback, unclear next steps. These are the friction points costing you users.

Key steps:

  • Core user journey mapping
  • Friction point identification
  • Error and edge case analysis
  • Mobile vs desktop experience gaps

Deliverables

Annotated user flows problem inventory

Recommendations

06

Organizing fixes by impact and effort

We compile everything into a report that groups issues by severity and effort to fix. Quick wins that boost conversion immediately. Medium-term improvements. Long-term redesign considerations. You know exactly what to tackle first.

Key steps:

  • Issues ranked by impact and complexity
  • Specific design and UX recommendations
  • Before/after examples where helpful
  • Implementation difficulty estimates

Deliverables

UX audit report prioritized action plan

Implementation plan

07

Turning findings into an execution plan

We work with your team to create a phased implementation plan. What gets fixed in the next sprint, what needs design resources, what requires development work. You walk away with a clear path forward, not just a list of problems.

Key steps:

  • Phased rollout plan
  • Resource and timeline estimates
  • Success metrics for tracking improvements
  • Follow-up audit recommendations

Deliverables

Implementation roadmap tracking framework

FAQ

Questions already
answered
01
What is a UX audit?

A UX design audit is a systematic evaluation of your product’s user experience. It examines usability, navigation, and interface consistency to pinpoint areas that need improvement. A well-conducted UX audit service ensures a smoother, more intuitive user journey, reducing friction and enhancing engagement.

02
Why does my business need UX audit services?

Investing in a UX audit company helps eliminate design inefficiencies, improve user satisfaction, and increase conversions. A thorough UX audit process uncovers pain points in user flows, allowing businesses to make data-driven improvements that enhance usability and engagement.

03
How to conduct a UX audit?

The UX audit process involves multiple stages, starting with analyzing key user interactions and evaluating the structure of your digital product. It includes a competitive audit UX to benchmark your product against industry standards, as well as testing usability across devices and platforms to identify areas for optimization.

04
What deliverables are included in the UX audit?

Our UX audit service provides a detailed UX Audit Report, including usability assessments, pain point analysis, and recommendations for improvement. We also offer a UI UX audit breakdown with prioritized action items, wireframes, and mockups where necessary.

05
How to do a UX site audit?

A website UX audit involves evaluating page structure, content organization, and user navigation. The process includes examining bounce rates, engagement patterns, and conversion bottlenecks. A strong audit UX design approach ensures that users find what they need effortlessly, improving retention and usability.

06
How much do UX audit services cost?

A professional UX audit agency typically charges $2,000 and up, depending on the complexity and scale of the project. The final cost varies based on the number of pages/screens analyzed, the depth of competitive analysis, and additional features such as audit UX UI refinements.

07
How long does a UX audit take?

The timeframe for a UX UI audit depends on the scope and complexity of your product. A standard UX audit service can take anywhere from a 5 days to a few weeks, ensuring comprehensive testing, competitive benchmarking, and in-depth user analysis.
